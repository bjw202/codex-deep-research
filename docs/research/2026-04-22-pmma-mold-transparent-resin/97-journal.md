# Journal Pass 1

## Research Design
Four researcher artifacts were treated as input only, with no web browsing or external verification. The journal compresses findings across PMMA mold feasibility, transparent resin candidates, PMMA forming routes, and surface/release/optical durability, while preserving uncertainty and conflict tracking for later critic review.

## Researcher Summaries
- R1: PMMA is only viable as tooling in low-heat, low-stress, chemically gentle processes. Best fits were UV imprinting and brief low-temperature casting, with PMMA framed as prototype or short-run tooling.
- R2: Resin family choice dominates compatibility. Room-temperature clear urethanes and clear epoxies look most PMMA-friendly; UV acrylates are faster but shrinkier; polyester is riskiest; silicone is gentle but too soft for rigid replicas.
- R3: PMMA replication is strongest in thermal embossing and thermal NIL, with injection compression as a higher-throughput option. Master quality, release treatment, and process temperature are the main determinants of fidelity.
- R4: PMMA surface energy is moderate, not naturally release-safe. Thin fluorinated or plasma-polymeric release layers are favored, while optical QA must include haze, transmittance, roughness, birefringence, and demolding damage.

## Key Claims And Numbers
- PMMA normal use cited around 70-80 C, with forming around 150-160 C; vulnerability to solvents such as ketones and esters is a repeated constraint.
- PMMA surface tension / surface free energy is reported in the roughly 39.5-45 mN/m range.
- UV acrylate shrinkage varies widely by formulation, from very low values to about 1.9% linear shrink in the cited examples.
- Clear epoxies cited include solvent-free optical grades with Tg around 157 C and UV-curable clear systems with about 0.3% shrinkage.
- Clear urethane casting resins are described as room-temperature, low-viscosity, and negligible-shrink options.
- PMMA embossing examples include 115 C / 34.3 MPa / 5 min and 200 C / 20 bar / 20 min conditions, with a reported 40-cycle mold life in one embossing study.
- Optical PMMA examples cite transmittance around 92.4% and haze around 0.18% for one film grade.

## Preliminary Conflicts
- PMMA is described both as viable tooling and as a fragile substrate. The reconciliation is process scope: short, low-heat, chemically mild runs versus sustained heat or solvent exposure.
- UV-curable systems are presented as good for speed but also as more shrink-prone and oxygen-sensitive than epoxies.
- Silicones are chemically favorable yet mechanically mismatched for rigid optical replicas, so “best chemistry” does not equal “best part type.”
- Surface-energy figures vary by grade and method, so release conclusions are directional rather than exact.
- Release coatings look promising, but optical contamination risk remains unresolved without process-specific validation.

## Synthesis Notes
The working synthesis is that PMMA can support transparent-resin replication only inside a narrow envelope: low temperature, low chemical aggression, low stress, and controlled demolding. The likely candidate order for a first-pass trial is room-temperature clear urethane or clear epoxy, then UV-curable clear systems if shrink and oxygen sensitivity are acceptable, with PMMA forming routes and release strategy chosen to match feature scale and cycle count.

The main unresolved tension is whether the final process is a one-off optical prototype, a short-run mold, or a repeated production tool. That choice changes which claims matter most: thermal ceiling, release durability, or replication fidelity.
